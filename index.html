Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Amazon Prime really cost for students? +

For eligible students and young adults (18-24), Amazon Prime costs just $7.49/month after your 6-month free trial. This is a significant discount compared to the regular $17.99/month price. You'll get all the same benefits including free shipping, Prime Video, music, and more.

What are the best Prime deals for students right now? +

Students can save hundreds on textbooks, electronics, and dorm essentials. Look for Prime-exclusive deals on Amazon's student deals page. Popular categories include laptops, study supplies, dorm decor, and fitness equipment. Prime members also get early access to Lightning Deals.

Can I share my Prime benefits with roommates or family? +

Prime for Young Adults does not include Amazon Household sharing benefits. Unlike regular Prime, you cannot share your Prime benefits with other household members. Each eligible student or young adult needs their own Prime for Young Adults membership to access the discounted benefits.

What's the best way to use Prime Video as a student? +

Prime Video is great for study breaks and downtime. Download movies and shows for offline viewing when you have spotty dorm WiFi. Look for educational documentaries, foreign films for language classes, and binge-worthy series to unwind after exams.

How do Prime savings add up over a school year? +

Students typically save $100-200 per semester on shipping alone. Add in Prime-exclusive deals, Whole Foods discounts, and Grubhub+ savings, and you're looking at $300+ in annual savings. Plus, the entertainment value of Prime Video and Music is priceless for dorm life.

What happens if I forget to cancel before my free trial ends? +

When your 6-month free trial ends, Amazon will automatically charge you the discounted student rate of $7.49/month for the next membership period. You can cancel anytime through your account settings, but paid members who haven't used their benefits are eligible for up to a full refund of the current membership period.

Can international students get the Prime student discount? +

Yes! International students enrolled in accredited educational institutions are eligible. You'll need to provide proof of enrollment through Amazon's verification process. The discount applies worldwide, though some benefits like free shipping may have regional restrictions.

What's the difference between Prime and Prime Student? +

Great question! There's no separate "Prime Student" plan anymore. All eligible students and young adults (18-24) automatically get the discounted $7.49/month rate. You get 100% of Prime benefits at a much lower price - same shipping, video, music, and everything else.

How do I verify my student status for the discount? +

You can verify through Amazon's student portal using your school email (.edu) or by uploading proof of enrollment like a student ID, schedule, or acceptance letter. The verification process takes just a few minutes and the discount applies immediately once approved.

When will I be billed for my Prime membership? +

When your 6-month free trial or membership period ends, Amazon will automatically charge for the next membership period at the discounted student rate of $7.49/month. You can cancel anytime through your account settings, but billing occurs automatically at the end of each membership period.

How does the 5% cash back work with Prime for Young Adults? +

Eligible Prime for Young Adults members (ages 18-24 or enrolled students) can earn 5% cash back on eligible Amazon.com purchases. This is a great additional benefit that adds up quickly on textbooks, electronics, and other student essentials. Cash back is subject to Amazon's terms and conditions.

What are the additional Prime benefits I get? +

Beyond the core benefits, Prime for Young Adults includes Prime Reading (hundreds of books monthly), Amazon Photos (unlimited storage), Prime Gaming (free games and Twitch subscription), early access to deals, free same-day pickup at Whole Foods, and more. You get all the same benefits as regular Prime members.
